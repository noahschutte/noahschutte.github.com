About Me

I'm a New York-based product manager working on two startup projects. I have experience advising an agile lean startup and I am proficient with HTML / CSS and JavaScript.

Three years of management consulting drove me into a profound quarter-life crisis. When I started to be honest with myself about what I wanted to do, I thought back to when I was the most proud of my work: as an entreprenuer in high school.

You can read about my childhood here, but please get in contact with me regarding the startups I've recently been working for in New York.

In the summer of 2002 I was 14 years old, and I was mowing lawns. It wasn't the smell of grass-clippings I enjoyed - I'm actually allergic. Or the heat exhaustion I would suffer from, working in temperatures above 100 degrees. I was proud of building a crew from my group of friends that dominated our neighborhood's semi-professional landscaping market niche.

We were called The Neighborhood Lawn Guys. After distributing nearly 300 flyers on our bikes, Danny and I built our book of clients. By the second summer I was managing the scheduling, billing, and payroll of our 4 staff members. So, when we chilled at the mall with our friends, we were looking fresh as hell in our air force ones.

But at 16, I started a more lucrative business. I charged $30 an hour to teach adult students computer skills. I built my book of clients by referrals.

When I'm not building exciting software products, I love to snowboard, solve puzzles, go camping, and I'll school you at bridge.
